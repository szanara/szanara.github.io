About

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science with a background in Mathematics, working at the intersection of machine learning, synthetic data, and robust AI.

My research focuses on data-centric approaches to improve model reliability, particularly through synthetic data generation and representation learning for wearable sensing and medical applications. I am especially interested in how models behave under limited, noisy, or adversarial conditions.

Ultimately, I aim to design machine learning systems that remain reliable in real-world scenarios, bridging the gap between theoretical understanding and practical deployment.
